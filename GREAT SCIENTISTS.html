20 GREATEST SCIENTISTS

Exploring the contributions of pioneering scientists who shaped our world

Albert Einstein

1879-1955

Developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. Best known for mass-energy equivalence formula E=mcÂ².

Marie Curie

1867-1934

Conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. First woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences.

Isaac Newton

1643-1727

Formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation. Developed calculus independently of Leibniz. Made crucial contributions to optics.

Charles Darwin

1809-1882

Proposed the theory of evolution by natural selection. His work established evolutionary descent as the dominant scientific explanation of diversity in nature.

Nikola Tesla

1856-1943

Designed the alternating current (AC) electrical system. Made contributions to the invention of radio, transformers, and wireless technology.

Galileo Galilei

1564-1642

Father of modern observational astronomy. Improved the telescope and supported Copernican heliocentrism. Formulated the basic principle of relativity.

Alan Turing

1912-1954

Father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence. Developed the Turing Machine concept and helped crack Nazi codes during WWII.

Rosalind Franklin

1920-1958

Made critical contributions to the understanding of DNA structure through X-ray crystallography. Her work provided key evidence for the double helix model.

Stephen Hawking

1942-2018

Theoretical physicist who made fundamental contributions to cosmology, especially black hole theory. Author of 'A Brief History of Time'.

Marie Maynard Daly

1921-2003

First African-American woman to earn a PhD in chemistry. Conducted important research on cholesterol, sugars, and protein synthesis.

Gregor Mendel

1822-1884

Discovered fundamental laws of inheritance through pea plant experiments.

Rosalind Franklin

1920-1958

Provided key evidence for DNA's double helix structure through X-ray diffraction.

James Watson

1928-Present

Co-discovered DNA's double helix structure, revolutionizing molecular biology.

Francis Crick

1916-2004

Co-discovered DNA's structure and formulated the central dogma of molecular biology.

Richard Feynman

1918-1988

Developed quantum electrodynamics and created Feynman diagrams.
